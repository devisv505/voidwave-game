// the job

Set up. Dig in. Hold.

You're a lone operator dropped onto a hostile world with orders that fit on one line. There's metal in the ground and that's why you're here.

So you start small — mine some ore, smelt it, lay a few belts so the work runs itself while you keep digging. But the planet isn't as empty as the survey said. After a while the ground tears open and things come out of it, and they come for you. So you build a wall of turrets and you hold. Then they come again, more of them, and you build more.

That's the whole loop: mine, build, automate, survive the next wave — then do it bigger. The factory grows because the threat grows. That's the story.

  • 1,000sof items crawling your belts from drill to gun
  • 3belt tiers — MK1 cyan, MK2 yellow, MK3 orange
  • 5-tiertech tree, from hand-drill to laser defense
  • EndlessSurges — and the less stable the rift, the harder they hit

// the loop

Build, stabilize, hold — then dive deeper

Every world is the same shape: a factory you grow and a rift you tame. Beat one and you drop into the next — deeper, darker and hungrier.

01

Build a factory

Drop in with a hand-drill and build out from nothing — mine copper and iron, smelt it, lay belts, and let smelters and assemblers turn ore into parts. Everything you do next runs on what this factory makes.

02

Stabilize the rift

At the heart of every world is a rift — an unstable black hole tearing the place apart. Build Stabilizer Cores, lock four of them into its ring, and start the charge to bring it under control.

03

Hold the line

The moment that charge starts, the Void comes for it — and the shakier the rift, the harder it hits. Auto-targeting turrets, your Pulse Blaster and the chain-lightning Arc Rifle buy the seconds you need to keep it charging.

04

Dive to the next world

Stabilize it and the rift opens into a shaft you can walk through. Dive — and drop into a new world: deeper, darker, with richer ore and a meaner Void. Then you build it all again.

// features

An honest factory game with teeth

Build a factory that runs itself

Lay belts and the work runs without you — ore crawls from the drills to the smelters, parts roll off the assemblers, and your turrets stay fed while you're off fighting in the dark. Splitters and mergers braid the lines; faster belt tiers keep the whole sprawl moving as your base grows.

  • Three belt tiers — MK1, MK2, MK3 — each faster than the last
  • Splitters, mergers and storage to shape the flow
  • Machines that auto-run their recipes straight off your belts

Power your tech with the enemy

Every creature the Void spits out drops a Void Core — a crystallized shard that fuels your research and your best gear. The deeper you climb the 5-tier tech tree, the more cores it costs. So progress literally requires killing more of the thing trying to kill you.

  • Survive → Arm → Hold → Dominate: a survival beat-sheet as a tech tree
  • Higher tiers gated behind Void Cores you harvest from waves
  • A clean reason to fight, not just turtle

A glitch in reality

There are no monster sprites. The Void comes as glitching wireframes torn into magenta, cyan and white — wobbling like a broken signal bleeding through the world. Cyan is yours. Magenta is theirs. The whole screen tells you who's winning.

  • A pitch-dark world lit only by your lamp, your machines and the rifts
  • Cyan is your machinery; magenta is the Void — read the fight at a glance
  • Rifts you can't close — the dark is here to stay
