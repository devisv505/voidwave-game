// the job

Set up. Dig in. Hold.

You're a lone operator dropped onto a hostile world with orders that fit on one line. There's metal in the ground and that's why you're here.

So you start small — mine some ore, smelt it, lay a few belts so the work runs itself while you keep digging. But the planet isn't as empty as the survey said. After a while the ground tears open and things come out of it, and they come for you. So you build a wall of turrets and you hold. Then they come again, more of them, and you build more.

That's the whole loop: mine, build, automate, survive the next wave — then do it bigger. The factory grows because the threat grows. That's the story.

  • 1,000sof items moving on belts at a steady 60 FPS
  • 3belt tiers — MK1 cyan, MK2 yellow, MK3 orange
  • 5-tiertech tree, from hand-drill to laser defense
  • ~25sbetween Surges — the Void never stops coming

// the loop

Four things, forever, bigger each time

01

Mine

Equip the wrist-mounted Hand Miner and pull copper and iron from a deterministic, hand-seeded world. Slow, but it needs no power — it's how every run begins.

02

Build & Automate

Smelters, constructors, assemblers, splitters, mergers, storage. Lay belts and let the factory refine ore into parts while you go do something that matters.

03

Defend

Auto-targeting gun and laser turrets hold the line. Personal weapons — the Pulse Blaster and chain-lightning Arc Rifle — handle whatever breaks through.

04

Survive the Surge

Every ~25 seconds the ground tears open and the Void pours out. Survive a wave, earn the night — then it comes back bigger, and so does your factory.

// features

An honest factory game with teeth

Belts that actually scale

The whole point is moving thousands of items at once without the framerate flinching. VOIDWAVE is engineered around it — items and machines are pure data ticked by one system, so your sprawl stays smooth as it grows across three belt tiers.

  • MK1 → MK2 → MK3 belts: 0.5, 1.5 and 3.0 cells/sec
  • Splitters, mergers, storage and auto-running machine recipes
  • Built for 60 FPS with a screen full of throughput

Power your tech with the enemy

Every creature the Void spits out drops a Void Core — a crystallized shard that fuels your research and your best gear. The deeper you climb the 5-tier tech tree, the more cores it costs. So progress literally requires killing more of the thing trying to kill you.

  • Survive → Arm → Hold → Dominate: a survival beat-sheet as a tech tree
  • Higher tiers gated behind Void Cores you harvest from waves
  • A clean reason to fight, not just turtle

A glitch in reality

There are no monster sprites. The Void renders as glitching wireframes torn with VHS-tearline channels — magenta, cyan, white — wobbling per vertex like a signal that shouldn't be there. Cyan is yours. Magenta is theirs. The whole screen tells you who's winning.

  • Custom unlit shaders, a dark void overlay, and a player lamp
  • Cyan infrastructure vs. magenta corruption — readable at a glance
  • Rifts you can't close — the threat is permanent by design
